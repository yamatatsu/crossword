## 1. Word Selection

### Good Words
- Common English words a native speaker would know (OCEAN, BRIDGE, TIGER, DREAM)
- A mix of word lengths: some 3-4 letter, some 5-6 letter, a few 7+ letter
- Words from diverse categories (nature, food, science, culture, daily life)
- Words with high-frequency letters (E, A, R, S, T, N) to facilitate intersections

### Avoid
- Obscure abbreviations (ABBR, MISC)
- Proper nouns unless very well-known (PARIS is ok, PYONGYANG is not)
- Slang that may not age well
- Profanity, offensive, or culturally insensitive terms
- Plurals or verb forms added solely to fit the grid (-ING, -TION padding)
- More than 2 three-letter words (too many short words feel cheap)

### Intersection-Friendly Letters
When choosing words, prefer those with vowels and common consonants at positions likely to intersect. Letters like J, Q, X, Z make intersection extremely difficult.

## 2. Clue Writing

### Principles
- **One clue, one fair path to the answer.** The solver should be able to reach the answer through reasoning, not lucky guessing.
- **Concise.** Under 80 characters. Every word earns its place.
- **No direct repetition of the answer word** in the clue text.

### Clue Types (mix these for variety)
| Type | Example (Answer: BRIDGE) |
|------|--------------------------|
| Definition | "Structure spanning a river" |
| Fill-in-blank | "London ___ is falling down" |
| Synonym | "Span or overpass" |
| Associative | "Card game for four players" |
| Wordplay/double meaning | "Dental work or a river crossing" |

### Good vs Bad Clues
| Quality | Clue for APPLE |
|---------|----------------|
| Good | "Fruit that kept the doctor away" |
| Good | "iPhone maker, or a pie filling" |
| Mediocre | "A type of fruit" (too vague) |
| Bad | "Red or green thing" (could be anything) |
| Bad | "APPLE" (never repeat the answer) |

### Rules
- Clue and answer must match grammatically (if clue implies plural, answer is plural)
- Avoid "A ___" or "The ___" as clue starters unless it's a fill-in-blank
- Use question marks for puns or misdirection: "Good with numbers?" → ACCOUNTANT

## 3. Grid Design

### Structure Principles
- **Symmetry is preferred** but not required for small grids. 180-degree rotational symmetry is the NYT standard.
- **Connectivity**: Every white cell must be reachable from every other white cell through adjacent white cells. No isolated islands.
- **Black cell ratio**: Aim for less than 35% black cells. Too many black cells = fragmented, unsatisfying puzzle.
- **No unchecked cells** (cells that belong to only one word) — ideally every white cell is part of both an across and a down word. In small puzzles this is hard, but minimize unchecked cells.

### Layout Patterns That Work Well
- **Scaffold pattern**: 2-3 long across words as the skeleton, connected by down words
- **Interlocking pattern**: Words woven together in a dense grid with minimal black cells
- **Stack pattern**: Long words stacked vertically or horizontally with short connectors

### Common Pitfalls
- Words running parallel and adjacent without black cell separation (creates forced letter sequences)
- Large open areas of black cells (wasted space, ugly)
- A single word connected to the rest of the puzzle by only one intersection (fragile, unsatisfying)

## 4. Theme & Cohesion

### Daily Puzzle Themes
A good daily puzzle has optional light theming. Ideas:
- **Seasonal**: Spring words in March, winter words in December
- **Day of week**: Monday = easier/lighter, Friday = harder
- **Current events**: Subtle nods to holidays, seasons, or widely-known events
- **Category cluster**: 3-4 words loosely related (e.g., all animals, all colors) while the rest are general fill

### Theme is Optional
Not every puzzle needs a theme. A well-constructed themeless puzzle with interesting words and clever clues is perfectly good.

## 5. Difficulty Calibration

### Target: Intermediate
- A competent English speaker should solve it in 5-15 minutes
- Most clues have a clear path; 1-2 clues may require some thought
- No clue should require specialist knowledge

### Difficulty Levers
| Lever | Easier | Harder |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Clue directness | "A fruit" | "Newton's inspiration" |
| Word frequency | HOUSE | ABODE |
| Clue type | Definition | Wordplay/misdirection |
| Grid density | More intersections (more cross-checking letters) | Fewer intersections |
